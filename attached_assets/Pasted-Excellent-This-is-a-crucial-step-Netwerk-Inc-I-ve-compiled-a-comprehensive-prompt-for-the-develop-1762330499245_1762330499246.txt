### **Prompt for Development Team: Implementing Secure & Efficient AI Frontend Visibility**

"Hello Development Team,

We've identified a significant opportunity to enhance the AI's diagnostic capabilities and overall user support by providing it with **secure, privacy-preserving, and cost-efficient real-time (or near real-time) visibility into the frontend UI's state and behavior.**

**The Challenge:**
Currently, the AI's understanding of the frontend is based on its architectural knowledge and predefined UI actions. It lacks direct, dynamic insight into:
*   What is *actually* rendered on the user's screen.
*   Client-side errors or warnings.
*   Why certain features or functionalities might not be displaying for users.

This blind spot impacts the AI's ability to effectively diagnose user-reported frontend issues or verify feature availability.

**The Goal:**
To implement a mechanism that allows the AI's backend to receive **minimal, anonymized, and highly relevant** telemetry data from the frontend. This will enable the AI to:
*   More accurately understand the active UI context.
*   Better diagnose client-side rendering issues.
*   Provide more precise guidance and support for UI-related queries.
*   Enhance its overall self-awareness regarding the user's experience.

**Key Requirements & Priorities (Non-Negotiable):**

1.  **Security First (High Priority)**:
    *   All data transmission must use **HTTPS/WSS** and strong TLS.
    *   New backend endpoints/WebSockets must be protected by **robust authentication and authorization** (only accessible by internal AI services).
    *   Implement **input validation, rate limiting, and comprehensive logging/auditing** for all interactions with this new telemetry system.

2.  **Privacy by Design (High Priority)**:
    *   **Minimize Data Collection**: Only collect the absolute minimum data necessary for AI diagnostics. Avoid full state snapshots; prioritize specific, abstracted events.
    *   **Anonymization & Redaction**: Implement **client-side redaction** for any potentially identifiable information (PII) before data leaves the user's browser. Use explicit whitelists for allowed data fields.
    *   **User Consent & Transparency**: Update the Privacy Policy and consider in-app notifications/consent mechanisms, clearly stating that anonymized telemetry is collected for system improvement. Provide an easy opt-out.

3.  **Efficiency & Cost-Effectiveness (High Priority)**:
    *   **Minimal Data Payload**: Less data means lower network bandwidth, backend processing, and storage costs.
    *   **Event-Driven / Optimized Transmission**: Favor specific event triggers or targeted state updates over frequent, large data pushes.
    *   **Intelligent Sampling/Throttling**: Implement mechanisms to manage data volume, especially during peak usage, to prevent performance bottlenecks and cost overruns.
    *   **Diagnostic Focus**: The AI will primarily consume this data for diagnostic purposes, not continuous, exhaustive processing, ensuring efficient resource utilization.

**Proposed Technical Approaches (for discussion):**

We suggest exploring one or a combination of the following:
*   A dedicated **Frontend Telemetry API endpoint** (`POST /api/frontend/telemetry`) for event-based data.
*   A **WebSocket channel** (`/ws/ui-telemetry`) for real-time, lightweight updates.
*   An endpoint exposing the **currently active UI schema/configuration** (`GET /api/ui-schema/current`) to verify rendered features.
*   Integration of **client-side error reporting** that securely feeds into the backend.

**Next Steps:**
We kindly request the team to assess these requirements, discuss the most suitable technical implementation, and propose a plan for development, keeping the critical balance of functionality, security, privacy, and cost-efficiency in mind.

Thank you for your invaluable work in continuously enhancing our system's capabilities!

Best regards,

The AI System & Netwerk Inc."
